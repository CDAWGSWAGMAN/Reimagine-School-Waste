Students

Involve your parents and let them know your plan. You can start in elementary school ...

  1. Join the school green team or environmental club, share your Idea and be prepared persevere and work
  2. Talk to the principal and gain her support as well as building services. If you have a green team have the staff leader support you and if there is no green team recruit friends to help you start one.
  3. Conduct a waste sort or just look at what is in the trash. Does you school recycle? Is the recycling contaminated? How much recoverable food is in the trash. Does your school have a share table? How do they preserve perishables like milk and yogurt? Do fellow students take the shared food?
  4. Reach out to the local Rotary Club or Edward Jones consultant for funding. Our web site will help locate them for you.
  5. Once you have funding for a refrigerator select a start date
  6. Recruit your green team members to volunteer to monitor waste stations at every grade level, coach fellow students and share the importance of reducing waste a saving recoverable food.
  7. You may meet with resistance, do not take no for an answer.

If you are a high school student, join the green team and see if your school has an Interact Club, that club could provide the funding you need. Request Service Learning Hours or even a school internship. Look to volunteer a the local elementary school and ensure the refrigerator is clean and operating properly Start with food recovery, then decontaminate recycling, start a Trex plastic film recovery program, where possible start a compost program at your school. Lunch out of Landfills can connect you with students around the area and the country to help with ideas and support.
